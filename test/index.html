Lecture 1: Software Development Life Cycle

Overview Lecture PDF Key Concepts Resources

Overview

The Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) is a process used by the software industry to design, develop and test high quality software. The SDLC aims to produce high-quality software that meets or exceeds customer expectations, reaches completion within times and cost estimates.

Exam Tip: Remember the order of stages: Requirement Analysis, Design, Implementation, Testing, Deployment, and Maintenance.

Key Concepts & Notes

  • Waterfall Model

    Linear sequential approach. Each phase must be completed before the next begins.

  • Agile Methodology

    Iterative approach. Focuses on customer satisfaction through continuous delivery.

  • DevOps

    Combines development (Dev) and operations (Ops) to shorten the systems development life cycle.
